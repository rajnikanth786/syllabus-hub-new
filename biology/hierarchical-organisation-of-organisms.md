## **1. Introduction: What Does ‚ÄúMulticellular‚Äù Mean?**

A **multicellular organism** is a living thing made up of **many cells** (sometimes millions, sometimes trillions!).
Humans, plants, mushrooms, dogs, birds, and insects ‚Äî they are all multicellular.

Unlike single-celled organisms (like bacteria), multicellular organisms have:

* **specialised cells**
* **different jobs for different cells**
* **structures that work together** to keep the organism alive

This organisation follows a **hierarchy** ‚Äî a system where smaller parts build up progressively into larger, more complex structures.

---

# **2. The Hierarchy Overview**

Multicellular organisms are organised in the following order:

```
Cells ‚Üí Tissues ‚Üí Organs ‚Üí Organ Systems ‚Üí Organism
```

Each level depends on the one before it.

To understand this, think of a **school**:

* A **student** is like a cell
* A **class** (group of similar students) is like a tissue
* A **department** (English, Science) is like an organ
* A **group of departments working together** is like an organ system
* The **whole school** is the organism

---

# **3. Cells ‚Äì The Basic Unit of Life**

A **cell** is the smallest unit that can carry out all the functions of life.

### **3.1 Types of Cells**

In animals:

* **Nerve cells** ‚Äì send electrical signals
* **Muscle cells** ‚Äì contract to create movement
* **Red blood cells** ‚Äì carry oxygen
* **White blood cells** ‚Äì fight infections

In plants:

* **Root hair cells** ‚Äì absorb water and minerals
* **Palisade cells** ‚Äì carry out photosynthesis
* **Guard cells** ‚Äì control gas exchange through stomata

### **3.2 Specialisation**

Cells become specialised through **differentiation**, meaning they develop structures that suit their function.

Example:

* A nerve cell is *long and thin* so signals can travel quickly
* A red blood cell has *no nucleus* to carry more oxygen

---

# **4. Tissues ‚Äì Groups of Similar Cells Working Together**

A **tissue** is a group of **similar cells** performing a **shared function**.

### **Examples in Animals**

| Tissue            | Function                                                         |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Muscle tissue     | Moves the body using contractions                                |
| Nervous tissue    | Sends and receives messages                                      |
| Epithelial tissue | Covers surfaces and protects organs                              |
| Connective tissue | Supports, connects, or holds things together (e.g., bone, blood) |

### **Examples in Plants**

| Tissue           | Function                           |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Xylem            | Carries water from roots to leaves |
| Phloem           | Transports sugars                  |
| Epidermal tissue | Protects leaves and stems          |

---

# **5. Organs ‚Äì Groups of Tissues Working Together**

An **organ** is made of **several tissues** that work together to carry out a specific job.

### **Examples**

### *Human organs:*

* **Heart** ‚Äì pumps blood
* **Lungs** ‚Äì exchange gases (oxygen in, carbon dioxide out)
* **Stomach** ‚Äì breaks down food
* **Skin** ‚Äì protects the body and regulates temperature

**‚ù§Ô∏è Example: The Heart Contains Multiple Tissues**

* Muscle tissue ‚Üí contracts to pump blood
* Nervous tissue ‚Üí controls heartbeat
* Connective tissue ‚Üí supports structure
* Epithelial tissue ‚Üí lines chambers and blood vessels

### *Plant organs:*

* **Roots** ‚Äì absorb water
* **Stem** ‚Äì provides support, transports substances
* **Leaves** ‚Äì carry out photosynthesis

---

# **6. Organ Systems ‚Äì Groups of Organs Working Together**

An **organ system** is a group of organs that cooperate to perform a major life function.

### **Major Organ Systems in Humans**

| System       | Major Organs               | Main Function                            |
| ------------ | -------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Circulatory  | Heart, blood vessels       | Moves blood around the body              |
| Respiratory  | Lungs, trachea             | Breathes in oxygen, removes CO‚ÇÇ          |
| Digestive    | Stomach, intestines, liver | Breaks down food & absorbs nutrients     |
| Nervous      | Brain, spinal cord, nerves | Controls and coordinates body activities |
| Skeletal     | Bones, cartilage           | Provides support & protection            |
| Muscular     | Muscles                    | Enables movement                         |
| Reproductive | Ovaries/testes             | Produces offspring                       |
| Excretory    | Kidneys, bladder           | Removes waste                            |
| Endocrine    | Glands                     | Produces hormones                        |

### **Plant Organ Systems**

Plants mainly have:

* **Shoot system** ‚Äì stem, leaves, flowers
* **Root system** ‚Äì roots that anchor and absorb water

---

# **7. The Whole Organism**

When all organ systems work together **successfully**, they form an **organism** ‚Äî a complete living being.

Examples:

* A human
* A mouse
* An oak tree
* A sunflower
* A salmon

Each organism:

* Grows
* Reproduces
* Responds to the environment
* Uses energy
* Keeps internal conditions stable (homeostasis)

---

# **8. Quick GCSE Check: Why Do Organisms Need This Structure?**

The hierarchical system allows organisms to:

* Be more efficient
* Specialise cells to perform specific tasks
* Grow larger and more complex
* Maintain homeostasis
* Carry out many processes at the same time (digestion, thinking, breathing)

A single cell alone **cannot** do all of these efficiently.

---

# **9. Example Walkthrough: How a Message Travels From Your Eye to Your Hand**

1. **Cell level:** Light hits *photoreceptor cells* in your retina
2. These form the **nervous tissue** in your eye
3. The retina is part of an **organ** (the eye)
4. The eye is part of the **nervous system**
5. The nervous system communicates with the **muscular system**
6. Your whole **organism** (you!) reacts by moving your hand

This shows how each level contributes to performing a life function.
