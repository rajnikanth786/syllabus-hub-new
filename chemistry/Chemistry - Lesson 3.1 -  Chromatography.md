Chromatography is a **separation technique** used to split a mixture of colours or chemicals into individual substances.

* Separating ink colours in pens
* Testing food dyes

A **chromatogram** is the **final result** produced after chromatography.

# ğŸ§© **How Chromatography Works**

1. A spot of the mixture is placed on **chromatography paper**.
2. The paper is dipped into a **solvent** (e.g., water).
3. The solvent travels up the paper by **capillary action**.
4. Substances in the mixture **separate** because they travel at **different speeds**.
5. The pattern of spots produced is the **chromatogram**.

---

# ğŸ¯ **Why Do Substances Separate?**

Each substance has different:

* **Solubility** (how well it dissolves in the solvent)
* **Attraction to the paper**

A highly soluble substance moves **further** up the paper.
A less soluble substance moves **a shorter distance**.

# ğŸ”¢ **Rf Values â€” Identifying Substances**

The **Rf value** helps compare chromatograms and identify substances.

### Formula:

[
\text{Rf} = \frac{\text{Distance moved by substance}}{\text{Distance moved by solvent}}
]

### Example:

* Substance moved: **4 cm**
* Solvent front: **8 cm**

[
Rf = \frac{4}{8} = 0.5
]

Each substance has a **known Rf value** in a given solvent, so scientists compare their results to data tables.
