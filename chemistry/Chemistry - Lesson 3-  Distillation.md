# ğŸ§­ **Step-by-Step: How Distillation Works**

## **1. Heating the mixture**

The mixture is placed in the boiling flask and heated.
As the temperature rises, the liquid with the **lowest boiling point** evaporates first.

## **2. Evaporation**

The evaporating substance changes from **liquid â†’ gas**.
The thermometer shows when this substance is boiling.

## **3. Vapour enters the condenser**

The hot vapour travels up and into the condenser.

## **4. Cooling and condensation**

Cold water constantly flows around the outside of the condenser.
This cools the vapour, turning it back into a **liquid**.

## **5. Collection**

The pure condensed liquid (called the **distillate**) drips into the receiving flask.

# ğŸ§ª **Types of Distillation**

### **1. Simple Distillation**

Used when separating:

* A liquid from a solid (e.g., salt water)
* One liquid from another when their boiling points are very different

### **2. Fractional Distillation**

Used when separating:

* Mixtures of many liquids (e.g., crude oil)
* Liquids with **similar boiling points**

A **fractionating column** improves separation.
